Tiramisu Recipe

What is Tiramisu?

Tiramisu is a popular Italian dessert that translates to "pick me up" in Italian. It is a delightful and classic dessert made with layers of coffee-soaked ladyfingers and a mascarpone cheese mixture. Tiramisu is often dusted with cocoa powder and may be garnished with chocolate shavings. It is known for its rich, creamy, and coffee-flavored taste.

Ingredients:

  • • 6 egg yolks
  • • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • • 1 cup mascarpone cheese
  • • 1 1/2 cups heavy cream
  • • 1 cup brewed espresso, cooled
  • • 1/4 cup coffee liqueur (optional)
  • • 24-30 ladyfingers
  • • Cocoa powder, for dusting
  • • Chocolate shavings (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a heatproof bowl, whisk together egg yolks and sugar over a double boiler until thick and pale.
  2. Remove from heat and whisk in mascarpone cheese until smooth.
  3. In a separate bowl, whip the heavy cream until stiff peaks form.
  4. Gently fold the whipped cream into the mascarpone mixture until well combined.
  5. In a shallow dish, combine brewed espresso and coffee liqueur (if using).
  6. Dip each ladyfinger briefly into the espresso mixture and arrange them in a single layer at the bottom of a serving dish.
  7. Spread half of the mascarpone mixture over the ladyfingers.
  8. Repeat the layers with the remaining ladyfingers and mascarpone mixture.
  9. Cover and refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight.
  10. Before serving, dust with cocoa powder and garnish with chocolate shavings if desired.
